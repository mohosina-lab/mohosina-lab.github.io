ABOUT

Hello, I'm Mohosina.

I am an early-career researcher and digital health professional with an interdisciplinary background in biomedical engineering, information technology, and healthcare implementation. My research focuses on machine learning, responsible AI, and digital health, with the goal of developing trustworthy AI systems that support evidence-based clinical decision-making.

I recently completed my Master's degree in Biomedical Engineering at the University of Oulu, Finland, where I worked as a Research Assistant in the Software Engineering and Information Systems (SEIS) research group. My research investigated predictive machine learning models for patient selection in spinal cord stimulation for chronic pain treatment, emphasizing model evaluation, risk stratification, and responsible use of clinical data.

Before transitioning into academic research, I spent nearly a decade working in digital health and healthcare organizations, leading projects related to health information systems, digital interventions, and technology implementation. These experiences continue to shape my research interests by connecting technical innovation with real-world healthcare challenges.

My long-term research interests include Responsible AI, healthcare AI governance, clinical decision support, machine learning, digital health, and health information systems. I am particularly interested in developing AI solutions that are transparent, trustworthy, and aligned with clinical practice and healthcare policy.

RESEARCH

Current Research

Patient Selection using Machine Learning

My current research focuses on evaluating the predictive performance of classical machine learning models for patient selection in Spinal Cord Stimulation (SCS) treatment for chronic pain. I compare multiple algorithms using expert-assigned appropriateness ratings to assess their predictive accuracy and robustness under controlled experimental conditions. In addition to predictive performance, I investigate how missing data and label uncertainty influence model reliability, with the broader aim of understanding the strengths and limitations of machine learning approaches for supporting future clinical decision-support research.
